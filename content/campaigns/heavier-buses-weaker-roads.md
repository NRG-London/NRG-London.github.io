## The research

Electric buses are heavier than the diesel vehicles they replace. That additional weight has a dramatic impact on road wear — and the costs fall on borough councils, not on TfL.

The headline finding: **switching one diesel single-decker to an electric equivalent adds road wear equivalent to 13,000 extra petrol cars.** This is a consequence of the fourth-power law of road damage, where doubling axle weight increases road wear by a factor of sixteen.

## The three-strand action plan

### 1. Sutton Council
Calling on the council to assess the impact on local road maintenance budgets and ensure TfL contributes to repair costs for routes served by heavier electric buses.

### 2. TfL and the Mayor
Pressing TfL through the Assembly's scrutiny process to acknowledge the road damage externality and factor it into the electric bus transition plan.

### 3. National press
Bringing the story to a wider audience to ensure the hidden costs of bus electrification are part of the public debate.
