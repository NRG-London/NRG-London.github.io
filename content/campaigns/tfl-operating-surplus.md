## The problem

TfL regularly claims to run an "operating surplus." This figure is misleading — it excludes substantial costs including debt servicing, capital renewals, and other items that any honest accounting would include.

## The campaign

I'm pursuing this through multiple channels simultaneously:

- **Ernst & Young** — Letter to TfL's auditors asking whether the "operating surplus" figure meets accepted accounting standards
- **Office for Statistics Regulation** — Formal request to examine whether TfL's public use of this figure is misleading
- **BBC Radio 4 More or Less** — Pitch to the programme to investigate the claim
- **Parliamentary scrutiny** — Engagement with relevant parliamentary figures

## Why it matters

When TfL claims a surplus while simultaneously requiring billions in government support, something doesn't add up. Londoners deserve honest numbers about the finances of their transport network.
